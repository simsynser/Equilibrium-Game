Preface

EQUILIBRIUM is an educational game developed to familiarize high school students and students at universities with basic concepts of systems thinking and causal thinking. I suggest embedding the game in teaching subjects related to systems science like geography, ecology or biology. The provided materials cover a full lesson:

  1. Students are introduced to basic concepts of causality, feedback and its formalization using Causal Loop Diagrams (see Chapter 1)
  2. Students play the game
  3. Students are provided with the key concepts and theories like environmental awareness, dynamic equilibrium, stability conditions and delay in systems (see Chapter 2)

Alternatively, the materials may be used as a flipped classroom activity where students play the game at home.

Among the many relevant aspects of system thinking, the game is particularly dedicated to address the following skills (see also Richmond and Peterson 2001):

  • Causal Thinking: Definition and conditions of causality (e.g. direction and polarity of causality).
  • Big Picture Thinking: Filtering of essential causal relations and parameters
  • Structural Thinking: Interpretation of system structure and behavior
  • Communication of Systems Structure: Creation and interpretation of Causal Loop Diagrams
